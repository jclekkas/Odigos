That suggestion matters because it turns Odigos from a tool people use privately into a tool that can spread itself.
Right now the flow is mostly:

user has concern → runs analysis → gets value → leaves

That can make money, but it does not compound very well.

A shareable deal scorecard changes the loop to:

user has concern → runs analysis → gets value → shares result with partner/friend/group chat/Reddit → new people discover Odigos

That is a very different business dynamic.

What a shareable scorecard is
It is a clean, visual summary of the result someone got from Odigos.
Not the full analysis. Not all the reasoning. Just the most share-worthy part.

For example:

This dealer quote triggered 3 red flags

Missing full out-the-door price
Add-ons not clearly optional
Doc fee appears high for this state
Verdict: Proceed carefully
That can live as:

an image card
a copyable text block
a share link with a preview image
a downloadable PNG
Why this is high leverage
Three reasons.
First, it creates distribution. People already ask friends and family, “does this deal look sketchy?” If Odigos gives them something polished to forward, the product enters that conversation naturally.

Second, it creates social proof. A vague AI output is forgettable. A crisp scorecard feels more concrete and credible.

Third, it creates identity. People do not just share tools. They share “look what this found” moments.

That is how a utility becomes a brand.

What it should look like
The first version should be simple.
A card with:

Odigos logo
headline like “Dealer Quote Check”
overall verdict: Good / Caution / Walk Away
2–4 top issues
short footer: “Analyzed by Odigos”
That is enough.
Do not over-design it. The goal is clarity, not decoration.

What not to do
Do not expose the full private quote details.
Do not include personally identifying information.
Do not dump too much text on the card.
Do not make it look like a marketing banner.
It should feel like a receipt of insight.

Best use cases
This would be powerful in:
iMessage with spouse or parent
Slack with coworkers
Reddit post screenshots
X/Twitter posts
Facebook groups
car-buying forums
A buyer is often not deciding alone. This gives them something easy to bring into that group decision.

The share card should contain:
verdict
top 3 issues
no dealer name unless user opts in
no sensitive quote details
That is enough to test whether people actually use it.
