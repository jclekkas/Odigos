Keep the existing site navigation, footer, dark theme, and the standard CTA block at the bottom:
“Already have a dealer quote? Find out if it’s a good deal. Paste your dealer message → Analyze”
Use this H1:
Dealer Added Fees After Agreement? What to Do Next
This page should be written in Odigos voice: calm, practical, independent, not ranty, not generic blog filler.
Structure the page as a comprehensive article with these sections:
Intro
Write an opening section for the moment when a buyer thinks the price is settled, then the dealer adds fees later in the process. Explain that this often happens after a verbal agreement, text quote, worksheet, or even after time has already been invested. Clarify that taxes and government fees are expected, but surprise dealer charges are a different issue.
Section: “Why dealers add fees after the agreement”
Explain clearly that some dealers separate the negotiation into stages so the buyer mentally commits before seeing the full out-the-door number. Cover tactics like:
introducing add-ons late
slipping in protection packages
reframing optional items as standard
relying on buyer fatigue after a long negotiation
Make this practical and buyer-focused, not accusatory.
Section: “Which fees are legitimate and which are red flags”
Differentiate between:
sales tax
title and registration
dealer documentation fee
versus fees that should trigger questions, such as:
paint/fabric protection
VIN etching
nitrogen-filled tires
theft recovery systems
reconditioning/prep fees
market adjustment / ADM
Explain that a fee is not automatically legitimate just because it appears on paperwork.
Section: “What to say when the price changes”
Include a copy-paste message template in a clearly styled block. Use this exact wording:
“Thanks. Before I move forward, I need the updated out-the-door price in writing with every added fee itemized. Please separate taxes and government fees from dealer-installed products or optional add-ons. If anything was added after our earlier quote, please show exactly what changed and why.”
After the block, explain why this works: it is specific, calm, and forces clarity.
Section: “What if they say the fees are non-negotiable?”
Explain that the buyer still has options:
ask which items are government-required versus dealer-added
ask for removal of optional products
negotiate the vehicle price down to offset fixed dealer charges
walk away if the breakdown is still vague
Section: “How to protect yourself before you go in”
Reinforce that the best defense is the full OTD in writing before visiting the dealership. Mention that if the number changes later, the buyer has a clean comparison point.

Closing
End by reinforcing the product:
If the buyer already has a text, quote, or worksheet with changed numbers, Odigos can analyze what’s there, what’s missing, and what to ask next before signing.
