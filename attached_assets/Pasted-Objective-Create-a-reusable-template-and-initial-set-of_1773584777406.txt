Objective
Create a reusable template and initial set of high-intent “scenario pages” for Odigos. These pages target real situations car buyers encounter when dealing with dealerships (price changes, hidden fees, finance office changes, refusal to give OTD pricing, etc.).

Strategic context
Odigos is consumer protection software for car buyers. The analyzer helps detect hidden dealer fees, missing out-the-door pricing, forced add-ons, and negotiation traps before a buyer visits the dealership.

These scenario pages capture high-intent searches where a buyer is already experiencing a problem and needs immediate clarity. Each page should explain the situation, provide guidance, and clearly present the Odigos analyzer as the tool that helps the reader verify their quote.

Constraints
• Do NOT add new product features
• Do NOT modify backend logic or routing
• Do NOT introduce email capture or new forms
• Only create new SEO article pages and follow the existing Odigos article layout patterns
• Maintain a calm, authoritative tone (not alarmist or sensational)

Page structure (required for every scenario page)

1. H1 title describing the situation clearly
   Example: “Dealer Changed the Price After a Deposit — What You Can Do”

2. Direct answer block immediately under the H1 (40–60 words)
   This should clearly answer the situation for Google snippet eligibility.

3. Section: What’s happening
   Explain the situation and why dealerships sometimes do this.

4. Section: Is it legal or common?
   Clarify whether the behavior is typical, legal, or questionable.

5. Section: What you should do next
   Provide practical guidance and negotiation framing.

6. Section: What to say to the dealer
   Provide a short script or phrasing buyers can use.

7. Contextual CTA block introducing Odigos
   Example:
   Headline: “Want to see exactly what changed?”
   Copy: “Paste the quote or message you received. Odigos flags missing out-the-door pricing, hidden fees, and dealer tactics.”
   Button: “Check My Deal” (link to /analyze)

8. Optional short FAQ section (2–3 questions if relevant)

Content quality rules
• Avoid generic filler text
• Use concise explanations and practical advice
• Keep paragraphs short (2–4 sentences)
• Avoid repeating the same language across pages
• Each page should feel tailored to the specific situation
