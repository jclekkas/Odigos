Replace the body content on /monthly-payment-trap with a comprehensive article. Keep the existing CTA at the bottom and site navigation/footer.

Use the H1: "The Monthly Payment Trap: How Dealers Use Payment-Focused Quotes to Hide the Real Cost"

Structure the article with these sections:

**Intro:** When a dealer asks "What monthly payment are you looking for?" instead of discussing the total price, that's a negotiation tactic, not a helpful question. Payment-focused selling shifts your attention away from the numbers that actually matter.

**"How the payment trap works":** Use a concrete example — a $489/month payment could mean ~$29,340 total at 4.9% for 60 months, or ~$35,208 at 7.9% for 72 months. Same monthly number, nearly $6,000 difference. Dealers can also hit a lower payment by extending the term to 72 or 84 months, which means more interest and higher risk of being underwater on the loan.

**"What payment-focused quotes typically hide":** APR (if they say it "depends on credit" without giving a range, they're leaving room to mark it up), dealer add-ons rolled into the payment without itemization, the loan term, and the total sale price.

**"How to avoid the trap":** Negotiate the out-the-door price first, then discuss financing separately. These are two different transactions. Request the full OTD in writing before visiting. Link to /out-the-door-price here. If the dealer refuses to give a total price and keeps redirecting to the payment, that's a red flag.

Include 1 outbound link to Edmunds supporting a specific claim. Add 1 internal link to /out-the-door-price (cornerstone). End with a clear path back to the tool: if you already have a payment-focused quote, Odigos can analyze what's missing.

## Prompt 3: Expand /dealer-wont-give-otd

Replace the body content on /dealer-wont-give-otd with a comprehensive article. Keep the existing CTA at the bottom and site navigation/footer.

Use the H1: "What to Do When a Dealer Won't Give You the Out-the-Door Price"

Structure the article with these sections:

**Intro:** You've found a car, you text or email the dealer asking for the out-the-door price, and instead of a straight answer you get: "Come on in and we'll work the numbers" or just a monthly payment with no breakdown. This is common, and it's not accidental.

**"Why dealers avoid giving the OTD":** A written OTD makes comparison shopping easy, locks them into a number, and removes their ability to use payment-based tactics. Dealers prefer to negotiate in isolation where you can't easily compare.

**"What to say when they dodge the question":** Include this copy-paste template: "Before I come in, I'd like to see the full out-the-door price in writing, including the sale price, sales tax, title and registration, doc fee, and any dealer-installed add-ons. If add-ons are included, please itemize each one with pricing. I'm ready to move forward once I can review the complete breakdown." Explain why it works: it's specific, reasonable, and signals you're an informed buyer.

**"What if they still won't?":** Try other dealers — most metro areas have multiple dealerships selling the same models, and many will compete on price over text or email. A dealer who won't be transparent before you visit is unlikely to become more transparent in the finance office.

**"How Odigos helps":** If you've received a partial quote — a monthly payment, a vague "around $35,000," or a worksheet with missing line items — Odigos analyzes exactly what's there and what's missing. You get a clear list of gaps and a suggested follow-up message.

Include 1 outbound link to FTC or Edmunds supporting a specific statement. Add 1 internal link to /out-the-door-price (cornerstone). Keep the tone practical and action-oriented — this page serves people in a moment of frustration.
