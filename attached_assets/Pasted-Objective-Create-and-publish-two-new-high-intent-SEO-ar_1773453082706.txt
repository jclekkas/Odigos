# Dealer Won’t Give an Out-the-Door Price? What to Do

If a dealer refuses to give you the out-the-door price (OTD) in writing, that's usually a red flag.

The out-the-door price is the total amount you will actually pay to drive the car off the lot. It includes the vehicle price, taxes, registration, documentation fees, and any add-ons.

Without this number, you cannot compare deals or understand the real cost of the car.

Dealers sometimes avoid giving the OTD price because it removes their ability to add fees later in the process.

## Why Some Dealers Avoid Giving the OTD Price

There are a few common reasons dealerships hesitate to provide the full out-the-door number.

### They want you to focus on the monthly payment

Dealers often steer the conversation toward monthly payments instead of the total price. This makes it easier to hide fees inside financing.

### Fees can be added later in the finance office

Some dealerships wait until the final paperwork stage to introduce additional charges such as:

• documentation fees  
• dealer-installed add-ons  
• protection packages  
• VIN etching or nitrogen tires  

If you only know the vehicle price, these costs can appear later.

### They want you physically at the dealership

Many dealers prefer to discuss final pricing in person. Once you're at the dealership and invested in the process, it's harder to walk away.

## What the Out-the-Door Price Should Include

A legitimate OTD price should include:

• vehicle sale price  
• sales tax  
• title and registration  
• dealer documentation fee  
• any required state or local fees  
• any add-ons already included in the deal  

If a dealer gives you only the vehicle price or a vague estimate, you don't yet know the real cost.

## What to Say If a Dealer Won’t Give the OTD Price

A simple message often works:

“Before I come in, could you send the full out-the-door price including taxes, registration, and all dealer fees?”

If they still refuse, it may be worth reconsidering that dealership.

Transparent dealers have no problem providing this number.

# Dealer Added Fees After We Agreed on Price — Is That Legal?

Many car buyers experience the same frustrating situation.

You agree on a price with the dealership, only to see new fees appear when it's time to sign the paperwork.

This often happens during the final finance step, when additional charges are introduced that were not clearly discussed earlier.

## Why Dealers Add Fees Late in the Process

In many cases, the initial agreement focuses only on the vehicle price.

The final contract may then include additional items such as:

• dealer documentation fees  
• protection packages  
• paint protection  
• VIN etching  
• extended warranties  
• gap insurance  

Some of these are optional products that were never clearly explained.

Others are legitimate fees but were not included in the earlier quote.

## Is It Legal?

In most cases, dealerships are allowed to charge legitimate fees, but the issue is transparency.

Problems arise when:

• fees appear that were never disclosed  
• optional products are presented as mandatory  
• the total price changes after an agreement  

While laws vary by state, misleading pricing practices can violate consumer protection rules.

## How to Protect Yourself

Before agreeing to any deal, ask for the full out-the-door price in writing.

That number should include:

• vehicle price  
• taxes  
• registration  
• dealer fees  
• any add-ons already included  

If something new appears later, ask for clarification before signing anything.

You always have the option to walk away.

so you can spot problems before finalizing the deal.
